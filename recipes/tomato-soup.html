Tomato Soup

Description

Soups are a wonderful way to eat vegetables and stay warm during winter. Tomato soup in particular is wonderful, as it has a very unique umami taste, with the cooked tomato and the vegetables.

Just eating it by itself is good enough, but it pairs very well with a grilled cheese sandwich. You dip it, then you eat it, and it tastes wonderfully.

Ingredients

  • Canned tomatoes
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Sugar
  • Oil
  • (Optional) carrots, celery and/or bell pepper
  • (Optional) smoked paprika, basil, and whatever spice you like
  • (Optional) vegetable broth
  • (Optional) some kind of alcohol, like cheap wine.

Steps

  1. Mise en place: get everything ready before starting to cook; chop the onion, garlic, and the rest of the vegetables. They don't need to be small, so you can just cut them into big chunks, but as you like. Open the tomato cans, and have all the ingredients to hand.
  2. Heat a pot on medium-high and throw some oil once it's hot. Then, throw the garlic. Once it starts to "dance" (which you will notice because it seems like the garlic is like jumping) throw in the rest of the vegetables. Stirfry until they are golden, or for as long as you like (making sure they don't burn).
  3. Now, throw in the tomatoes and the broth, and season with salt, pepper and a teaspoon of sugar (this is to avoid it from getting too sour). Once you threw in the tomatoes, turn down the fire and fill the cans with water, shake them and throw in the liquid (it will have lots of extra tomato chunks).
  4. Cover with a lid and cook on low heat for at least 20 minutes.
  5. Use a blender or food processor to blend the soup until it's nice and creamy.
  6. Before serving it, heat up some cream or cream cheese in the microwave, and drop a spoon of it into the soup. You can decorate it with some basil, and even eat it with a grilled cheese sandwich if you like. Enjoy!

Tips and warnings

  • If you want, you can deglaze the vegetables once ready, before throwing in the tomatoes. To do this, throw in a bit of the alcohol and scrape the bottom of the pot, and let the liquid evaporte (it won't take too long, don't worry).
  • While it's cooking, the soup might get very dry and stick to the pan. To avoid this, throw in some water during the cooking process if necessary.
  • You can actually freeze the soup and, whenever you need some, simply heat it up in the microwave.
